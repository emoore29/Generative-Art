Chance

In 2019, the incidence rate of acute lymphoblastic leukaemia in Australia for people between 20 and 39 was 0.8 per 100,000, or 0.0008%.

Between 2015 and 2019, the relative five-year ALL survival rate for people between 25 and 34 was 74.7%.

The artwork is designed to simulate these statistics. There are 101,761 light purple dots in the frame. A white dot indicates five-year survival of ALL diagnosis. If there is a loss, and one dot does not pass the 75% survival chance, then the remaining dots will be white.

The statistics were sourced from the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare.
